On July 29, MCPS released the raw results of the boundary options survey. A total of 1,170 individuals responded, representing a diverse mix of racial backgrounds, school clusters, and cities across the county. The overwhelming majority opposed each of the proposed boundary changes—less than 20% of respondents supported any option.

Top Themes

  • Opposition to reassignments from Churchill to Wootton: Families cite home buying decisions, proximity, and community ties.
  • Frustration with split feeder patterns: Especially Hoover MS students split between multiple high schools.
  • Proximity matters: Many prefer neighborhood schools and oppose long bus commutes.
  • Lack of clarity and trust in the planning process: Calls for transparency and more inclusive decision-making.

Notable Quotes

“There was no real opportunity to give feedback before the options were announced.”
“Splitting middle school kids is difficult socially and emotionally.”
“We live closer to Churchill but are being reassigned to Wootton — make it make sense.”

Option 1

Top Concerns

  • Reassigning Potomac residents from Churchill to Wootton.
  • Disruption of community cohesion and longer commutes.

Option 2

Top Concerns

  • Splitting Hoover MS into multiple high schools.
  • Loss of peer continuity and social impact on students.

Option 3

Top Concerns

  • Ignoring proximity by assigning nearby communities to distant schools.
  • Increased traffic and travel time.

Option 4

Top Concerns

  • Broad shifts across clusters with no clear justification.
  • Distrust in planning and equity concerns.
